Hans Matthew Riess

Bio

Hans employs algebraic, topological and geometric methods to pioneer advancements in machine learning, complex systems, and optimization. In 2016, Hans received a B.S. in pure mathematics from Duke University. In 2022, he received a Ph.D. in Electrical and Systems Engineering from the University of Pennsylvania where, with Robert Ghrist, he developed a network sheaf theory for algebraic lattices (pictured right). He is currently a postdoctoral associate in the Autonomous Systems Lab at directed by Michael M. Zavlanos at Duke University.
